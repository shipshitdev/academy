# Fundamental vs. Technical Analysis

## Learning Objective
Understand the two main approaches to analyzing markets: fundamental analysis (why prices move) and technical analysis (how prices move), and when to use each.

## Main Content

Traders use two main approaches to make decisions. Understanding both helps you develop a complete trading strategy.

**Fundamental Analysis**:

**What It Is**: Analyzing the underlying factors that affect an asset's value—economic data, company performance, news events, etc.

**Key Principles**:
- Assets have intrinsic value based on fundamentals
- Prices eventually reflect true value
- Focus on "why" prices should move
- Long-term perspective

**For Stocks**:
- Company financials (revenue, profit, debt)
- Industry trends
- Management quality
- Competitive position
- Economic conditions

**For Forex**:
- Economic indicators (GDP, employment, inflation)
- Central bank policies (interest rates)
- Political stability
- Trade balances
- Geopolitical events

**For Commodities**:
- Supply and demand
- Weather patterns (agriculture)
- Geopolitical tensions (oil)
- Economic growth (affects demand)

**Pros**: Understands underlying value, good for long-term, less affected by market noise.

**Cons**: Doesn't predict short-term moves, can be slow to react, requires extensive research.

**Technical Analysis**:

**What It Is**: Analyzing price charts and patterns to predict future movements based on historical behavior.

**Key Principles**:
- Price reflects all available information
- History tends to repeat
- Focus on "how" prices move
- Patterns and indicators guide decisions

**Tools Used**:
- Chart patterns (head and shoulders, triangles)
- Indicators (moving averages, RSI, MACD)
- Support/resistance levels
- Volume analysis
- Trend lines

**Pros**: Works for any timeframe, clear entry/exit signals, less research needed, can be automated.

**Cons**: Can be subjective, patterns don't always work, ignores fundamentals, can lead to overtrading.

**Which Approach to Use**:

**Use Fundamental Analysis If**:
- Trading long-term (weeks to months)
- Want to understand why markets move
- Trading stocks or forex based on economic data
- Have time for research

**Use Technical Analysis If**:
- Trading short-term (minutes to days)
- Want clear entry/exit signals
- Prefer visual analysis over research
- Trading any market type

**Combined Approach** (Best Practice):

Many successful traders combine both:
- **Fundamental**: Determine direction (bullish or bearish overall)
- **Technical**: Time entries and exits (when to buy/sell)

Example: Fundamental analysis says a company is strong (buy), but technical analysis shows it's overbought (wait for pullback before buying).

**Common Mistakes**:
- Ignoring fundamentals when trading news events
- Using technical analysis without understanding trends
- Switching approaches mid-trade (confusion)
- Not aligning approach with trading timeframe

**Developing Your Style**:

Try both approaches:
- Paper trade using only fundamentals for a month
- Paper trade using only technicals for a month
- Compare results and identify what fits your personality
- Combine what works best for you

## Key Takeaways
- Fundamental analysis focuses on "why" (economic data, company performance, news)
- Technical analysis focuses on "how" (charts, patterns, indicators)
- Fundamental is better for long-term; technical is better for short-term
- Combining both approaches often works best
- Choose an approach that matches your timeframe and personality
