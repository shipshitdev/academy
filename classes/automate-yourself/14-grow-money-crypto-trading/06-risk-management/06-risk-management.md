# Risk Management: Protecting Your Capital

## Learning Objective
Learn the essential risk management principles that protect your trading capital and are the foundation of long-term trading success.

## ⚠️ CRITICAL WARNING - READ THIS FIRST

Before we dive in, let's be crystal clear: **There is no free lunch. This is not going to be easy. Success is not guaranteed.**

Risk management requires real work, discipline, and consistent effort. More importantly, **YOU CAN LOSE YOUR CAPITAL. YOU CAN LOSE EVERYTHING.** Even with perfect risk management, you can still lose your entire account. Most traders lose money. Trading is extremely risky. This is not guaranteed to make money—it's guaranteed to be risky. Don't be stupid—only trade with money you can afford to lose completely, follow risk management strictly, stay disciplined, and understand that you could lose everything you put in.

## Main Content

Risk management is the most important skill in trading. Without it, you'll lose money no matter how good your analysis. Here's how to protect your capital:

**The Golden Rule**: Never risk more than you can afford to lose. Trading is risky—you can lose your entire account. Only trade with money you don't need for living expenses.

**Position Sizing**:

**The 1-2% Rule**: Never risk more than 1-2% of your account on a single trade.

Example: If you have $10,000:
- Risk per trade: $100-200 maximum
- If stop loss is $2 below entry price, you can buy 50-100 shares
- This ensures 50-100 losing trades before account is gone

**Why It Matters**: Even the best traders are wrong 40-60% of the time. Small losses are manageable; large losses wipe you out.

**Stop Losses**:

**What They Are**: Pre-set orders that automatically close your position if price moves against you by a certain amount.

**Why Essential**: Prevents small losses from becoming catastrophic. You decide your risk before entering, not during emotional moments.

**Setting Stop Losses**:
- Place below support (for longs) or above resistance (for shorts)
- Use ATR (Average True Range) for volatility-based stops
- Don't place stops too tight (you'll get stopped out by noise)
- Don't place stops too wide (you risk too much)

**Risk-Reward Ratio**:

**The Concept**: Your potential profit should be at least 2-3x your potential loss.

Example:
- Risk: $100 (stop loss distance)
- Reward: $300 (profit target distance)
- Ratio: 3:1 (risk $1 to make $3)

**Why It Matters**: Even if you win 40% of trades, a 3:1 ratio means you're profitable overall.

**Calculation**: If you win 40% of trades with 3:1 ratio:
- 4 wins × $300 = $1,200
- 6 losses × $100 = $600
- Net profit: $600

**Diversification**:

**Don't Put All Eggs in One Basket**:
- Don't risk everything on one trade
- Don't trade only one asset (diversify)
- Don't trade only one strategy

**Correlation**: If trading multiple positions, ensure they're not all correlated (won't all move together).

**Leverage and Margin**:

**Leverage**: Borrowed money to increase position size. Amplifies both gains and losses.

**Danger**: High leverage can wipe out accounts quickly. Example: 10:1 leverage means 10% move against you = 100% loss.

**Best Practice**: Start with low or no leverage. Increase only as you gain experience and consistent profitability.

**Risk Management Checklist**:

Before every trade:
- [ ] Risk is 1-2% of account maximum
- [ ] Stop loss is set and reasonable
- [ ] Risk-reward ratio is at least 2:1
- [ ] Position size matches risk parameters
- [ ] Not over-leveraged
- [ ] Not risking money needed for expenses

**Emotional Risk Management**:

**Trading Psychology**:
- Don't trade when emotional (angry, fearful, greedy)
- Don't revenge trade (trying to win back losses)
- Don't increase risk after losses
- Stick to your plan, even when it's hard

**Common Mistakes**:
- Moving stop losses further away (hoping it comes back)
- Adding to losing positions (averaging down)
- Risking too much on "sure thing" trades
- Ignoring risk management rules when on winning streak

**The Bottom Line**: Risk management isn't exciting, but it's what separates profitable traders from those who blow up accounts. Master this before focusing on advanced strategies.

## Key Takeaways
- Never risk more than 1-2% of account per trade
- Always use stop losses to limit losses
- Maintain at least 2:1 risk-reward ratio (risk $1 to make $2+)
- Don't use excessive leverage, especially as a beginner
- Only trade with money you can afford to lose completely
- Stick to risk management rules even when emotions tempt you to break them
