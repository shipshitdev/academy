# Store Setup Basics (Shopify)

## Learning Objective
Learn the essential steps to configure your Shopify store properly, from account setup to core settings that affect your business operations.

## Main Content

Proper setup prevents problems later. Here's what to configure when launching your Shopify store:

**Initial Setup Steps**:

**1. Choose Your Store Name**
Pick something memorable and brandable. Evaluate:
- Domain availability (check if .com is available)
- Social media handle availability
- Easy to spell and remember
- Reflects your niche or brand

**2. Configure Store Settings**
- **Store currency**: Match your target market
- **Store address**: Used for taxes and legal requirements
- **Contact information**: Customer support email
- **Time zone**: Important for analytics and scheduling

**3. Set Up Payment Methods**
- **Shopify Payments**: Enable if available in your country (lowest fees)
- **PayPal**: Popular, increases trust
- **Credit cards**: Essential for most customers
- **Alternative methods**: Apple Pay, Google Pay for convenience

**4. Configure Shipping Settings**
- **Shipping zones**: Where you'll ship (start with your target market)
- **Shipping rates**: Free shipping, flat rate, or calculated
- **Processing time**: Set realistic expectations (account for supplier lead times)
- **Dropshipping adjustment**: Add 2-3 days buffer for supplier processing

**5. Tax Settings**
- Enable automatic tax calculation
- Configure tax rates for your regions
- Set up tax exemptions if applicable
- Consult tax professional for your situation

**6. Legal Pages**
Create essential pages:
- **Privacy Policy**: Required by law
- **Terms of Service**: Protect your business
- **Refund Policy**: Clear return/refund process
- **Shipping Policy**: Set customer expectations

**7. Essential Apps**
Install these early:
- **Dropshipping app**: DSers, Spocket, Zendrop, CJdropshipping, or AutoDS
- **Email marketing**: Klaviyo or Omnisend
- **Product reviews**: Loox or Judge.me
- **Analytics**: Google Analytics and Meta Pixel

**8. Design Your Store**
- Choose a theme (free or paid)
- Customize colors and fonts to match your brand
- Add your logo and favicon
- Set up homepage sections

**9. Test Everything**
Before launching:
- Place test orders (use Shopify's test mode)
- Check checkout process
- Test payment methods
- Verify email notifications
- Check mobile responsiveness

**Common Mistakes to Avoid**:
- Skipping legal pages
- Unclear shipping times
- Missing contact information
- Poor mobile optimization
- No refund policy

## Key Takeaways
- Configure store settings, payments, and shipping before launching
- Set up legal pages (privacy policy, terms, refund policy) to protect your business
- Install essential apps early: dropshipping, email marketing, reviews
- Test everything with test orders before going live
- Set realistic shipping times accounting for supplier processing
