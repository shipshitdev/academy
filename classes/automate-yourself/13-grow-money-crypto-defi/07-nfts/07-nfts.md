## Main Content

NFT stands for Non-Fungible Token. "Non-fungible" means unique and not interchangeable. Unlike Bitcoin (where one Bitcoin equals another), each NFT is one-of-a-kind.

**What is an NFT?**

An NFT is a unique digital certificate of ownership stored on a blockchain. It proves you own a specific digital item (or right to something). The "item" itself might be an image, video, music file, in-game item, or even access to something physical.

**Key Concepts**:

**Ownership**: NFTs prove ownership of digital assets. Like a deed proves house ownership, NFT proves digital asset ownership.

**Blockchain Record**: Ownership is recorded on blockchain (usually Ethereum). This creates permanent, verifiable record.

**Metadata**: NFTs contain metadata (information about the item)—title, description, creator, properties, etc.

**Uniqueness**: Each NFT is unique with its own identifier. Even if image looks identical, NFTs are different tokens.

**How NFTs Work**:

**Minting**: Creating an NFT. Upload digital file, pay gas fees, NFT is created on blockchain with your address as owner.

**Buying/Selling**: NFTs are traded on marketplaces (OpenSea, Rarible, etc.). Transactions are recorded on blockchain.

**Smart Contracts**: NFTs use smart contracts that can include royalties (creator gets percentage of future sales automatically).

**Storage**: NFT points to where file is stored. Usually on IPFS (decentralized storage) or centralized servers. File itself isn't on blockchain (too expensive).

**Major NFT Use Cases**:

**Digital Art**:
- Artists sell digital artwork as NFTs
- Buyers own unique digital art
- Most famous: CryptoPunks, Bored Ape Yacht Club
- Digital art market exploded in 2021

**Collectibles**:
- Digital trading cards, profile pictures (PFPs)
- Sports moments, virtual items
- Similar to physical collectibles but digital

**Gaming**:
- In-game items as NFTs (ownable across games potentially)
- Virtual land in metaverse
- Character skins, weapons, etc.

**Music and Media**:
- Musicians selling albums/songs as NFTs
- Exclusive content access
- Royalty distribution

**Real-World Assets**:
- Property deeds
- Event tickets
- Identity verification
- Certificates and credentials

**Membership and Access**:
- NFT as membership card
- Access to exclusive communities
- Voting rights in DAOs (Decentralized Autonomous Organizations)

**How to Buy NFTs**:

**Step 1: Get a Wallet**
- MetaMask or similar Web3 wallet
- Fund with Ethereum (most NFTs are on Ethereum)

**Step 2: Choose a Marketplace**
- OpenSea (largest, most popular)
- Rarible, SuperRare, Foundation
- Each has different NFTs and features

**Step 3: Browse and Research**
- Check creator credibility
- Verify authenticity (beware fakes)
- Research floor prices and sales history
- Understand what you're buying

**Step 4: Make Purchase**
- Connect wallet to marketplace
- Place bid or buy at listed price
- Pay gas fees (can be expensive)
- NFT appears in your wallet

**NFT Market Reality**:

**The Hype**:
- NFTs exploded in popularity 2021
- Some sold for millions
- Celebrity and brand endorsements
- Massive media coverage

**The Reality**:
- Most NFTs lose value
- Market is highly speculative
- Many projects are scams or fail
- High gas fees make trading expensive
- Environmental concerns (Ethereum energy use)

**Risks and Considerations**:

**Volatility**: NFT prices can crash dramatically. What's worth thousands today might be worthless tomorrow.

**Scams**: Fake NFTs, rug pulls, phishing attacks are common. Research thoroughly.

**Gas Fees**: Ethereum fees can be $50-500+ per transaction. Makes small trades uneconomical.

**Liquidity**: Hard to sell NFTs quickly. No guaranteed buyers.

**Storage Risk**: If file is stored centrally, it could disappear. NFT ownership remains, but what you "own" might be broken link.

**No Intrinsic Value**: Unlike stocks (ownership in company) or crypto (usable technology), NFT value is purely what someone will pay.

**Environmental Impact**: Ethereum's energy consumption is criticized. Moving to proof-of-stake reduces this.

**Evaluating NFTs**:

**Creator**: Who created it? Do they have reputation?

**Community**: Is there active community? Check Discord, Twitter.

**Utility**: Does NFT provide access, rights, or benefits?

**Rarity**: How rare is this NFT in the collection?

**Art/Quality**: Do you actually like it? (if art-based)

**Sales History**: What have similar NFTs sold for?

**Roadmap**: Does project have plans? Are they executing?

**NFTs: Investment or Speculation?**

Most NFTs are speculation, not investment. Like collectibles, value depends on what others will pay. Some NFTs have utility (gaming, access), but most are purely speculative.

**Best Practices**:
- Only buy what you can afford to lose
- Research thoroughly before buying
- Understand what you're actually buying
- Don't FOMO into hyped projects
- Account for gas fees before small purchases

## Key Takeaways
- NFTs are unique digital certificates of ownership on blockchain
- Major use cases: digital art, collectibles, gaming items, membership, access
- NFTs are bought/sold on marketplaces like OpenSea using crypto wallets
- Most NFTs are highly speculative and lose value—research thoroughly
- High gas fees, scams, and storage risks are significant concerns
- Only invest what you can afford to lose completely
