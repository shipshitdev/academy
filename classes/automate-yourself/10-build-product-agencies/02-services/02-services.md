# Defining Your Services

## Learning Objective

Learn how to define clear, valuable AI automation services that solve real problems and generate revenue.

## Main Content

Your services are what you sell. Define them poorly and you can't sell or deliver. Define them well and you have a clear value proposition and delivery process.

**The Service Definition Framework:**

**1. Problem-Focused**

**Start with Problems:**
- What problems do clients have?
- What tasks are repetitive?
- What processes are inefficient?
- What's costing time/money?

**Problem Examples:**
- "We spend 10 hours/week answering the same customer questions"
- "We manually process 100 invoices per week"
- "We can't keep up with content creation"
- "We lose leads because we don't follow up fast enough"

**2. Solution-Focused**

**Define Solutions:**
- How does AI automation solve the problem?
- What tools are used?
- What's the process?
- What's the outcome?

**Solution Examples:**
- "AI chatbot answers customer questions 24/7"
- "AI processes invoices automatically in minutes"
- "AI generates content based on your brand and topics"
- "AI qualifies and follows up with leads automatically"

**3. Outcome-Focused**

**Define Outcomes:**
- What results do clients get?
- What time is saved?
- What money is saved?
- What's the ROI?

**Outcome Examples:**
- "Save 10 hours/week on customer service"
- "Process invoices 10x faster"
- "Publish 5x more content"
- "Increase lead conversion by 30%"

**Service Definition Template:**

**Service Name:**
- Clear, descriptive name
- What it does
- Who it's for

**Problem:**
- What problem does it solve?
- Who has this problem?
- What's the cost of not solving it?

**Solution:**
- How does it work?
- What tools are used?
- What's the process?

**Outcomes:**
- What results do clients get?
- Time saved
- Money saved
- ROI

**Deliverables:**
- What does the client receive?
- Documentation
- Training
- Support

**Pricing:**
- How much does it cost?
- Pricing model
- Payment terms

**Service Selection Criteria:**

**Choose Services Based On:**
- Your skills and interests
- Market demand
- Competition level
- Profitability
- Scalability
- Delivery complexity

**Start with 1-3 Services:**
- Do not do everything
- Master a few services first
- Expand later
- Focus on quality

**Service Positioning:**

**General vs. Niche:**
- **General**: Multiple services, various clients
- **Niche**: Focused services, specific industry

**Both Work:**
- General: Easier to start, more competition
- Niche: Harder to start, less competition, higher rates

**Recommendation:**
- Start general, find niche
- Or start niche if you have expertise
- Specialize as you grow

**Pricing Models:**

**Project-Based:**
- Fixed price per project
- Clear scope
- One-time payment
- Good for specific deliverables

**Retainer:**
- Monthly fee
- Ongoing service
- Predictable income
- Good for maintenance

**Performance-Based:**
- Pay for results
- Higher risk/reward
- Requires proof
- Good for confident outcomes

**Hybrid:**
- Setup fee + monthly
- Common model
- Balances risk
- Scalable

**Documentation:**

## Key Takeaways

- Define services based on real problems, not just what you can do
- Focus on outcomes and value, not just features
- Start with 1-3 services and master them
- Use clear service definition template
- Choose a pricing model (project, retainer, performance, hybrid)
- Position services clearly (general vs. niche)
- Use AI tools to research, design, and document services
