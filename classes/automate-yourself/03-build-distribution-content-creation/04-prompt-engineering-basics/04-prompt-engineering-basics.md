# Prompt Engineering Basics

## Learning Objective
Master the fundamental techniques for writing effective prompts that produce high-quality, relevant AI-generated content.

## Main Content

Prompt engineering is the skill of crafting instructions that guide AI to produce exactly what you need. It's the difference between generic outputs and precisely tailored content.

**The Core Principles**:

1. **Be Specific**: Instead of "write about marketing," say "write a 500-word blog post about email marketing for small businesses, focusing on open rate optimization."

2. **Provide Context**: Give background information. "You are a marketing expert writing for entrepreneurs. Explain email marketing..."

3. **Define the Format**: Specify structure, length, tone, and style. "Write in a conversational tone, use bullet points, include a conclusion."

4. **Use Examples**: Show what you want. "Write in the style of: [paste example]" or "Use this format: Introduction, 3 main points, conclusion."

5. **Iterate and Refine**: First prompts rarely hit perfectly. Ask for revisions: "Make it more casual," "Add statistics," "Shorten paragraph 2."

**Common Prompt Patterns**:

- **Role-playing**: "You are a [profession] writing for [audience]..."
- **Chain of thought**: "First explain X, then analyze Y, finally conclude with Z"
- **Few-shot learning**: Provide 2-3 examples of desired output
- **Constraints**: "Under 300 words," "no jargon," "include 3 examples"

**Advanced Techniques**:
- Break complex tasks into steps: "Step 1: Research topic. Step 2: Create outline. Step 3: Write content."
- Use brackets for variables: "Write about [topic] for [audience]"
- Request thinking process: "Think step by step about..."

Remember: clarity beats cleverness. Well-structured, specific prompts consistently outperform vague, creative ones.

## Key Takeaways
- Specificity and context are essential for effective prompts
- Define format, tone, length, and style explicitly
- Use examples and role-playing to guide AI behavior
- Iterate and refine—first prompts are starting points, not final outputs
