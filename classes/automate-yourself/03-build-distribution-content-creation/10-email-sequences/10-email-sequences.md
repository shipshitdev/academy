# Project 4: Build Email Marketing Campaigns

## Learning Objective
Create complete email marketing sequences using modern tools, from welcome emails to nurturing campaigns and promotional sequences.

## Main Content

Email marketing remains one of the most effective channels. AI can help you create personalized, engaging email sequences at scale.

**Project: Create a 5-Email Welcome Sequence**

**Step 1: Define Your Sequence Goals**
What should this sequence accomplish?
- Welcome new subscribers
- Deliver value
- Build trust
- Guide toward first purchase/action

**Step 2: Map the Customer Journey**
Prompt: "Create a 5-email welcome sequence for [your business/niche]. Each email should have a specific purpose: 1) Welcome, 2) Share value/story, 3) Educate, 4) Social proof, 5) Call-to-action. Map the progression."

**Step 3: Write Email 1 - Welcome**
Prompt: "Write a welcome email for new subscribers to [your business]. Requirements: warm and personal tone, 100-150 words, thank them for subscribing, set expectations, include one clear next step or resource."

**Step 4: Write Email 2 - Your Story**
Prompt: "Write the second email in a welcome sequence. Share the story behind [your business/brand] in a relatable way. 150-200 words, personal tone, build connection, include why you started this journey."

**Step 5: Write Email 3 - Value/Education**
Prompt: "Write an educational email for [your audience]. Provide one actionable tip or insight related to [your niche]. 200-250 words, valuable content, include examples, friendly and helpful tone."

**Step 6: Write Email 4 - Social Proof**
Prompt: "Write an email featuring customer success stories or testimonials. Show how [your product/service] has helped others. 150-200 words, credible and authentic, include specific results or benefits."

**Step 7: Write Email 5 - Call to Action**
Prompt: "Write a final email in the welcome sequence with a clear call-to-action. Invite them to [specific action]. 100-150 words, compelling but not pushy, create urgency or offer value, friendly tone."

**Step 8: Create Subject Lines**
For each email:
Prompt: "Generate 5 subject line options for this email: [paste email]. Mix styles: curiosity, benefit-focused, personal, and direct. Keep under 50 characters."

**Step 9: Add Personalization Variables**
Replace generic terms with variables:
- [First Name]
- [Business Name]
- [Specific Interest]
- [Location] (if relevant)

**Best Practices**:
- Test subject lines
- Space emails 2-3 days apart
- Track open rates and adjust
- Maintain consistent voice throughout

## Key Takeaways
- Map out your email sequence goals before writing
- Each email should have a specific purpose in the customer journey
- Generate multiple subject lines and test what works
- Use personalization variables to make emails feel tailored
