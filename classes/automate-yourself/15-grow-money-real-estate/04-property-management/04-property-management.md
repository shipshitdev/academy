# Property Management: Running Your Rental Business

## Learning Objective

Learn how to manage rental properties effectively, including finding tenants, handling maintenance, and maximizing returns while minimizing headaches.

## Main Content

**What is Property Management?**

Property management is the operation, control, and oversight of rental property. It includes finding tenants, collecting rent, handling maintenance, and ensuring property remains profitable.

**Property Management Options:**

**1. Self-Management** (You Do It)

**Pros**:

- Save money (no management fees)
- Full control
- Learn the business
- Better understanding of property

**Cons**:

- Time-consuming
- Handle all problems
- Tenant issues
- 24/7 availability needed

**Best For**:

- First property (learning)
- Close to property
- Have time
- Want to save money

**2. Professional Property Management** (Hire Company)

**Pros**:

- Hands-off (less work)
- Professional experience
- Handle problems
- Better tenant screening

**Cons**:

- Costs money (8-12% of rent)
- Less control
- May not care as much
- Still need oversight

**Best For**:

- Multiple properties
- Don't have time
- Don't live nearby
- Want to scale

**3. Hybrid** (Mix of Both)

**What**: Do some yourself, hire for rest.

**Example**:

- You find tenants, screen
- Property manager handles maintenance, collection

**Property Management Tasks:**

**1. Finding Tenants**

**Marketing**:

- List on Zillow, Apartments.com, Craigslist
- Use AI to generate listings
- Take good photos
- Write compelling descriptions

**Screening**:

- Credit check
- Income verification (3x rent)
- Background check
- References
- Employment verification

**AI-Powered Screening**:

- AI analyzes applications
- Credit checks
- Background checks
- Risk assessment

**2. Lease Agreements**

**What to Include**:

- Rent amount and due date
- Security deposit
- Lease term
- Pet policy
- Maintenance responsibilities
- Rules and regulations

**Use**: Standard lease template (state-specific)

**Legal**: Consult lawyer for your state's requirements

**3. Rent Collection**

**Methods**:

- Online payment (Zelle, Venmo, apps)
- Automatic bank transfer
- Check (old school)

**Late Fees**: Set clear policy (5% after 5 days, etc.)

**AI Tools**:

- Automated rent collection
- Payment reminders
- Late fee calculations

**4. Maintenance and Repairs**

**Regular Maintenance**:

- HVAC servicing (yearly)
- Plumbing checks
- Appliance maintenance
- Landscaping
- Cleaning

**Repairs**:

- Emergency repairs (24/7)
- Routine repairs
- Capital improvements

**Costs**: Budget 10-15% of rent for maintenance.

**5. Tenant Relations**

**Communication**:

- Respond promptly
- Be professional
- Document everything
- Set boundaries

**Issues**:

- Complaints
- Requests
- Disputes
- Evictions (last resort)

**6. Financial Management**

**Track**:

- Income (rent)
- Expenses (maintenance, taxes, etc.)
- Cash flow
- Taxes

**Use**: Spreadsheet or property management software

**AI Tools**:

- Automated expense tracking
- Categorization
- Financial reports
- Tax preparation

**Finding Good Tenants:**

**The Screening Process:**

**1. Application**:

- Personal information
- Employment
- Income
- References
- Credit authorization

**2. Credit Check**:

- Credit score (650+ ideal)
- Payment history
- Debt load

**3. Income Verification**:

- Pay stubs
- Bank statements
- Employment verification
- Income should be 3x rent minimum

**4. Background Check**:

- Criminal history
- Eviction history
- Previous landlord references

**5. Decision**:

- Choose best qualified applicant
- Be consistent (fair housing laws)
- Document everything

**Red Flags**:

- Low credit score (<600)
- Income <3x rent
- Eviction history
- Poor references
- Criminal history (violent crimes)

**Maintenance Management:**

**Preventive Maintenance**:

**Schedule**:

- HVAC: Yearly service
- Plumbing: Check yearly
- Appliances: Service as needed
- Exterior: Inspect quarterly
- Interior: Inspect yearly

**Why**: Prevents expensive repairs, keeps tenants happy.

**Emergency Repairs**:

**24/7 Availability**: Need to respond to emergencies.

**Common Emergencies**:

- Water leaks
- No heat/AC
- Broken pipes
- Electrical issues
- Lockouts

**Have**: List of reliable contractors (plumber, electrician, HVAC, general)

**Maintenance Budget**:

**Rule**: 10-15% of rent for maintenance and repairs.

**Example**:

- Monthly rent: $2,000
- Maintenance budget: $200-300/month
- Annual: $2,400-3,600

**CapEx Reserve** (Major Repairs):

- Roof: $5,000-15,000 (every 20-30 years)
- HVAC: $3,000-8,000 (every 15-20 years)
- Appliances: $500-2,000 each (every 10-15 years)

**Budget**: 5% of rent for CapEx.

**Tenant Issues:**

**Common Issues**:

**1. Late Rent**:

- Set clear policy
- Send reminders
- Charge late fees consistently
- Document everything

**2. Complaints**:

- Respond promptly
- Investigate
- Fix legitimate issues
- Document

**3. Maintenance Requests**:

- Respond within 24-48 hours
- Prioritize (emergency vs. routine)
- Fix promptly
- Follow up

**4. Noise Complaints**:

- Review lease terms
- Address with tenant
- Document
- Evict if necessary

**5. Property Damage**:

- Document with photos
- Deduct from security deposit
- Repair
- Charge tenant

**Evictions** (Last Resort):

**When**:

- Non-payment of rent
- Lease violations
- Property damage
- Illegal activity

**Process**:

- Follow state laws (varies by state)
- Give proper notice
- File with court
- Get judgment
- Evict (sheriff)

**Legal**: Consult lawyer. Evictions are complex and vary by state.

**Maximizing Returns:**

**1. Increase Rent** (When Appropriate)

**When**:

- Market rates increased
- Lease renewal
- Improvements made
- Below market rent

**How**:

- Research market rates
- Give proper notice (30-60 days)
- Justify increase (if needed)

**2. Reduce Expenses**

**Ways**:

- Negotiate insurance rates
- Shop for contractors
- Do some maintenance yourself
- Preventative maintenance (avoid big repairs)

**3. Improve Property**

**Value-Add Improvements**:

- Renovations (kitchen, bathroom)
- Add features (washer/dryer, parking)
- Energy efficiency (lower utility costs)
- Curb appeal

**4. Minimize Vacancy**

**Ways**:

- Good tenants (longer stays)
- Quick turnarounds (repairs, cleaning)
- Competitive pricing
- Good marketing

**Common Management Mistakes:**

**Poor Tenant Screening**: Accepting bad tenants.

**Solution**: Screen thoroughly. Better to have vacancy than bad tenant.

**Not Responding**: Ignoring tenant requests.

**Solution**: Respond within 24-48 hours. Good service = good tenants.

**Deferred Maintenance**: Not fixing things.

**Solution**: Fix promptly. Small problems become big problems.

**No Systems**: Managing ad-hoc.

**Solution**: Create systems. Document everything.

**Not Tracking**: Don't know if profitable.

**Solution**: Track income and expenses. Know your numbers.

## Key Takeaways

- Property management is work—either do it yourself or hire manager
- Good tenant screening is critical—better vacancy than bad tenant
- Maintenance is ongoing—budget 10-15% of rent
- Respond to tenant issues promptly—good service = good tenants
- Track everything—know your income and expenses
- Use AI tools for screening, maintenance, and communication
- Document everything—protects you legally
- Systems matter—create processes for efficiency
